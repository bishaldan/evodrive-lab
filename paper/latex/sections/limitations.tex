\section{Limitations}

EvoDrive Lab is a simplified 2D benchmark and should not be interpreted as a realistic autonomous driving simulator. The benchmark scale is modest, the procedural suites are small, and the PPO baseline is intentionally lightweight rather than heavily tuned. The paper therefore focuses on comparative generalization trends inside this benchmark, not on real-world driving relevance or state-of-the-art claims.

The benchmark also omits several features that would matter in richer settings: traffic, perception noise, nontrivial vehicle dynamics, and broader hyperparameter sweeps. These omissions are deliberate, but they narrow the scope of the conclusions. The value of EvoDrive Lab lies in reproducibility, visibility, and compact comparative experimentation, not in environmental realism.
